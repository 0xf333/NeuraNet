27/04/2023
-
05:57
CDTCompartir en FacebookCompartir en TwitterCompartir en TelegramCompartir en WhatsappEnviar por emailCarlos Vela had a difficult match in the first leg of the CONCACAF Champions League semi-finals.LAFC could not take the lead, but it was enough to draw 1-1 in their visit to Philadelphia Union. The first finalist will be decided next Tuesday in the second leg in Los Angeles.But beyond that, Carlos Vela took several blows during the match, one of which led to shoving among several players.It was the 43rd minute when Norwegian Jakob Glesnes came out of his area with the ball, the Mexican attacker came in to cut the play, both players came off badly, but Vela got the worst of it. This led to a brawl between several players.Carlos Vela recovered and was able to continue in the match, but in the second half he was hit again when he was looking to counterattack.The Mexican player controlled the ball outside his area and advanced to Philadelphia's defensive zone until Olivier Mbaizo cut the play by colliding with the LAFC player, but referee Cesar Ramos, also Mexican, did not signal anything.Carlos Vela came off in the 69th minute for Biuk and that was the end of a tough game for the Mexican player.Carlos Vela - EnglishLos Angeles FC - English
